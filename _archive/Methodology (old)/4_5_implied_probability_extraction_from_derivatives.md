# 4.5 Implied Probability Extraction from Derivatives

This section describes how option-implied, risk-neutral probabilities are extracted from liquid Bitcoin options and aligned to the 5-minute master panel. These probabilities serve as a financial-market benchmark against which prediction market probabilities can be compared in later econometric and arbitrage analysis. The discussion reflects only what has been implemented and validated in the current pipeline; planned extensions are indicated explicitly. No empirical results are reported.

---

## 4.5.1 Risk-Neutral Probability from Options

Option-implied probabilities are computed from **Deribit BTC options**. The options are European-style, with expiries and strikes selected to align with the event set used for comparison (e.g., specific expiry dates and near-at-the-money strikes). The options panel that feeds this step is the same liquidity-filtered panel described in Section 4.2: only instruments that satisfy **liquidity inclusion criteria** (minimum nonzero volume bars and total volume thresholds) are retained, so that the input series reflect tradable prices rather than stale or illiquid quotes. Within that panel, **filtering for liquid near-ATM contracts** is applied by construction of the event set: for each target expiry and strike, the instrument used is the call (or, if unavailable, the put) whose strike is closest to the target within a narrow band, ensuring that the option used for implied volatility (IV) and probability is the most liquid for that event.

**Black–Scholes implied volatility** is estimated by inverting the Black–Scholes formula: given the observed option price, the spot (or index) price, the strike, and the time to expiry, the volatility parameter is found numerically so that the model price matches the market price. Both call and put options can be used depending on availability; the inversion is performed separately for each 5-minute bar where a valid option price and spot price exist. **Deriving the probability of finishing above the strike** uses the standard lognormal result: under the Black–Scholes assumptions, the risk-neutral probability that the underlying price at expiry exceeds the strike \(K\) is given by \(N(d_2)\), where \(d_2\) is the usual Black–Scholes argument (a function of spot, strike, time to expiry, and the implied volatility). This quantity is computed **per contract, expiry, and strike** at each timestamp: first IV is inferred from the option price, then \(N(d_2)\) is evaluated using the same spot, strike, time to expiry, and IV. The result is interpreted as the **risk-neutral probability benchmark** for the event “price above strike at expiry”—directly comparable to the corresponding prediction market probability for the same economic event.

**Alignment to the 5-minute panel** is achieved by merging option and master-panel data on the common 5-minute timestamp. Where the option series is sparse (no trade in a given bar), a limited **forward-fill of implied volatility** from recent bars may be applied so that the probability series has fewer gaps; the probability is then recomputed from the filled IV using the current spot and time to expiry. This fill is applied only within the same event (same expiry and strike), so that no cross-contract contamination occurs. The final output is a 5-minute series of option-implied probabilities per event, stored alongside event identifiers, implied volatility, and timestamps. **Option-implied probabilities** thus serve as the **financial-market reference** for prediction market probabilities: both refer to the same event (e.g., Bitcoin above a given strike at a given expiry), and the comparison is the basis for tests of consistency and arbitrage in later sections.

## 4.5.2 Probability Term Structures Across Expiries

The implemented pipeline uses **multiple expiries** (e.g., February and March expiries in the default event set) and **multiple strikes** per expiry (e.g., three near-ATM strikes). For each expiry, **probabilities are compared across strikes** at the same timestamp: the option-implied probability that price finishes above strike \(K_1\) should be no lower than the probability that price finishes above a higher strike \(K_2\). **Monotonicity diagnostics** are run to check that this ordering holds at sampled timestamps; any violations are recorded as part of the data-quality checks. This gives a minimal **term-structure** in the sense of a cross-section of probabilities across strikes within an expiry, and across expiries for the same strike, at each point in time.

(Planned extension — full term-structure modeling across maturities, including parametric or nonparametric smoothing of the probability surface over strike and time-to-maturity, may be developed in future work.)

## 4.5.3 Robustness and Data Quality Filters

**Liquidity inclusion criteria** are applied at two stages. First, the options data that feed the probability computation are drawn from a **liquidity-filtered options panel**: instruments must satisfy **minimum nonzero volume bars** and **total volume thresholds** over the sample so that only actively traded options are used. Second, after option-implied probabilities are computed per event, **coverage thresholds** are applied: for each event (expiry–strike pair), the share of 5-minute bars with a non-missing implied volatility (and hence non-missing probability) is computed. **Contracts with insufficient IV coverage** are flagged; in a subsequent cleaning step, **event-level filtering** is applied by **dropping event identifiers** that have zero or negligible IV coverage (e.g., no valid implied volatility over the sample). This yields a **cleaned event set** and a **cleaned probability series** that exclude low-quality or effectively untraded events.

**Sanity checks** are performed on the probability series before and after cleaning. **Probability bounds**: all option-implied probabilities are verified to lie in the unit interval \([0, 1]\); any value outside this range is treated as invalid and clipped or dropped in the cleaned output. **Strike monotonicity diagnostics**: at a sample of timestamps, the probabilities across strikes for a given expiry are checked so that higher strikes correspond to weakly lower probabilities; violations are counted and reported for quality assessment. **Expiry and time-to-maturity checks**: time to expiry is computed consistently (e.g., in years using a standard day-count), and bars with non-positive time to expiry (past expiry) are excluded or flagged so that the probability is defined only when the option is live.

The **final output** of this stage is a 5-minute panel of option-implied probabilities per event, written as **step3_probabilities_5m.parquet** (and a **cleaned** version that drops events with insufficient IV coverage). This dataset, together with the **cleaned event set** (list of event identifiers retained after coverage and sanity checks), forms the derivatives-based probability benchmark used for later comparison with Polymarket and for econometric tests in the remainder of the thesis.
